In ancient China it was known by three names: the sun dog (due to the yellowish-red color), the lion dog (thanks to the rich coat of arms) and the dog sleeve (because it was so small that it could be worn in the sleeves of the courtiers).

  • Scientific name: Pekingese
  • Average Lifespan: 11.4 years
  • Weight: from 3.2 to 6.4 kg

    • The Pekingese is a breed of Chinese dogs, known for over 4,000 years. It is called after Pekin, the former name of the capital of China, Beijing. In ancient China, it was reserved exclusively for the royal palace, cared for by the eunuchs, and the theft or the sale of a Pekingese were punished with death.
